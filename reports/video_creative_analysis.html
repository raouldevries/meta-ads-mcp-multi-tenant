Actionable Insights

🔴 Problems Identified

  • ! 56% drop in first 3 seconds - Hook isn't stopping the scroll. Question format may feel like an ad immediately.
  • ! 5s average watch time on a 30s video means most viewers never hear the key benefits.
  • ! Static single-shot for first 28 seconds - no visual variety to maintain interest.

🟢 What's Working

  • ✓ Strong retention after 7s - Those who stay are highly engaged (only 9% more drop-off for remaining 22s).
  • ✓ 11% complete the video - Good for 30s testimonial content.
  • ✓ 5.27% CTR - Above average, suggesting those who watch convert well.

💡 Recommendations

  • 1 New hook strategy: Start with the result ("I went skiing in Italy!") instead of a question.
  • 2 Add movement: Include B-roll of gym, equipment, or other members in first 5 seconds.
  • 3 Text hook: Add bold text overlay in first frame: "She lost 10kg in 3 months" or similar outcome.
  • 4 Shorter version: Test a 15s cut focusing on best moments (skiing mention + CTA).

📊 Benchmark Comparison

  • ✓ ThruPlay rate (11%): Industry average for 30s video is 8-12%. You're in range.
  • ! 3-second retention (44%): Top performers retain 60-70% at 3s. Room for improvement.
  • ✓ CTR (5.27%): Excellent! Average video ad CTR is 1-2%.

Executive Summary

This testimonial ad has strong conversion performance (5.27% CTR) but loses 56% of viewers in the first 3 seconds. The content quality is good—viewers who make it past 7 seconds stay engaged—but the hook isn't stopping the scroll effectively.

Priority fix: Restructure the opening to lead with an outcome/result rather than a question. The "skiing in Italy" moment at 16.7s would make a much stronger hook than the current question format.
